## 1. Introduction and Scope

This document governs the baggage policies applicable to all SkyItalia Airlines operated flights, including domestic Italian routes, intra-European Union routes, and intercontinental routes operated under the SkyItalia designator code (AZ). It applies to all passengers traveling on tickets issued by SkyItalia Airlines (ticket numbers beginning with 055), regardless of the point of sale or distribution channel used for booking.

The policies contained herein apply to flights operated by SkyItalia Airlines aircraft. For flights marketed by SkyItalia but operated by a codeshare partner, refer to Section 15 (Codeshare and Interline Baggage Rules). SkyItalia Airlines is a member of the SkyAlliance partnership and honors interline baggage agreements with all SkyAlliance member carriers, subject to the most-significant-carrier rule as established by IATA Resolution 302.

SkyItalia Airlines reserves the right to refuse any baggage that does not comply with the policies stated in this document, or that the airline considers potentially hazardous to the safety of the aircraft, its crew, or its passengers. In all matters of safety, the Captain's decision is final and binding.

All monetary values in this document are stated in Euros (EUR) and are inclusive of applicable taxes unless otherwise noted. Fees paid at the airport are subject to an additional service surcharge as indicated in the relevant fee tables.

## 2. Definitions

For the purposes of this document, the following definitions apply:

- **Cabin Baggage (Carry-on):** Baggage that the passenger carries into the aircraft cabin and stores in the overhead compartment or under the seat in front.
- **Personal Item:** A small bag (handbag, laptop bag, small backpack, or similar) that fits under the seat in front of the passenger.
- **Checked Baggage (Hold Baggage):** Baggage that is delivered to SkyItalia at check-in, transported in the cargo hold, and returned to the passenger at the destination.
- **Linear Dimensions:** The sum of length + width + height of a single piece of baggage, measured in centimeters.
- **Overweight Baggage:** A piece of checked baggage exceeding the standard weight limit of 23 kg but not exceeding 32 kg.
- **Oversized Baggage:** A piece of checked baggage exceeding 158 cm in linear dimensions.
- **Fare Type:** The pricing category of the ticket purchased (Economy Light, Economy Standard, Economy Flex, Premium Economy, Business).
- **Cabin Class:** The physical cabin in which the passenger is seated (Economy, Premium Economy, Business).
- **PIR (Property Irregularity Report):** The standard aviation industry form for reporting baggage irregularities (loss, delay, damage).
- **Montreal Convention:** The Convention for the Unification of Certain Rules for International Carriage by Air, signed in Montreal on 28 May 1999.
- **IATA:** The International Air Transport Association.
- **Most-Significant-Carrier (MSC):** The IATA rule that determines which airline's baggage policy applies on interline itineraries.
- **Through-Checked Baggage:** Baggage checked from the origin to the final destination, even when the itinerary involves one or more connections.
- **Dangerous Goods (DG):** Articles or substances which are capable of posing a risk to health, safety, property, or the environment, as classified by the IATA Dangerous Goods Regulations.
- **ULD (Unit Load Device):** An aircraft container or pallet used for loading and transporting cargo and baggage in the hold.

## 3. Cabin Baggage

### 3.1 Personal Item — All Classes

Every passenger on every SkyItalia flight, regardless of fare type or cabin class, is permitted to bring **1 personal item** free of charge. This includes a handbag, laptop bag, small backpack, briefcase, or similar small bag.

- **Maximum dimensions:** 40 x 30 x 15 cm (length x width x depth)
- **No weight limit** for the personal item
- Must fit entirely under the seat in front of the passenger
- During taxi, takeoff, and landing, the personal item must remain stowed under the seat; it may not be placed in the overhead compartment

A duty-free shopping bag (up to 1 standard-size bag) is permitted in addition to the personal item, provided the items were purchased after the security screening point at the departure airport or onboard a SkyItalia flight.

### 3.2 Carry-on Bag — Economy Class (Light, Standard, Flex)

Passengers holding an Economy class ticket (regardless of sub-fare: Light, Standard, or Flex) are permitted:

- **1 carry-on bag**
- **Maximum dimensions:** 55 x 40 x 20 cm (including handles, wheels, and external pockets)
- **Maximum weight:** **8 kg**

The carry-on bag must fit in the overhead compartment. SkyItalia employs sizing frames at check-in counters and boarding gates. If the bag does not fit the sizing frame, it will be checked to the hold. If checked at the gate and the passenger has no checked bag allowance (Economy Light), a gate-check fee of EUR 50 applies.

Note: On some aircraft in the SkyItalia fleet (particularly the Boeing 737-800), overhead bin dimensions may be slightly smaller than on the A320neo/A321neo fleet. Passengers are advised to adhere strictly to the 55 x 40 x 20 cm limits to avoid issues.

### 3.3 Carry-on Bag — Premium Economy

Passengers holding a Premium Economy ticket are permitted:

- **1 carry-on bag**
- **Maximum dimensions:** 55 x 40 x 20 cm
- **Maximum weight:** **10 kg**

Premium Economy passengers enjoy priority boarding (Group 2), which ensures overhead bin space is available when they board.

### 3.4 Carry-on Bag — Business Class

Passengers holding a Business Class ticket are permitted:

- **2 carry-on bags**
- **Each bag maximum dimensions:** 55 x 40 x 20 cm
- **Each bag maximum weight:** **10 kg each**

Business Class passengers enjoy priority boarding (Group 1) and dedicated overhead bin space in the Business cabin. Garment bags up to 57 x 54 x 15 cm are accepted as one of the two carry-on items.

### 3.5 Cabin Baggage — Summary Table

| Cabin Class | Personal Item | Carry-on Bags | Max Dimensions (per bag) | Max Weight (per bag) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Economy (Light/Standard/Flex) | 1 (40x30x15 cm, no weight limit) | 1 | 55 x 40 x 20 cm | 8 kg |
| Premium Economy | 1 (40x30x15 cm, no weight limit) | 1 | 55 x 40 x 20 cm | 10 kg |
| Business | 1 (40x30x15 cm, no weight limit) | 2 | 55 x 40 x 20 cm | 10 kg each |

### 3.6 Cabin Baggage — Operational Procedures

On all SkyItalia flights, cabin crew perform a pre-departure cabin inspection to verify that all carry-on items are properly stowed. If the overhead bins are full before all passengers have boarded, cabin crew will offer to gate-check excess carry-on bags to the hold free of charge, provided the passenger's fare includes checked baggage allowance. If the fare does not include checked baggage (Economy Light), the gate-check fee applies.

During periods of exceptionally high load factor (typically school holiday periods and major Italian public holidays), SkyItalia may enforce stricter cabin baggage controls. Passengers are encouraged to check in online and purchase checked baggage allowance in advance to avoid delays at the boarding gate.

The Captain reserves the right to order the removal of any cabin baggage item that, in the crew's judgment, poses a safety risk or cannot be properly secured.

## 4. Checked Baggage — Standard Allowances

The following section describes the default checked baggage allowance for each fare type. These allowances apply to SkyItalia-operated flights on all route types unless otherwise specified in Section 5.

### 4.1 Economy Light

- **Checked baggage: Not included.**
- **Add online (via skyitalia.com or SkyItalia app, at least 4 hours before departure):** **EUR 35** for 1 bag up to 23 kg.
- **Add at airport (check-in counter or self-service kiosk):** **EUR 50** for 1 bag up to 23 kg.
- Economy Light is designed for travelers with cabin baggage only. Passengers who require checked baggage are strongly encouraged to purchase it online in advance to benefit from the lower rate.

### 4.2 Economy Standard

- **1 checked bag included** (up to **23 kg**).
- Maximum linear dimensions: 158 cm (length + width + height).
- **Additional bag:** EUR 35 online, EUR 50 at airport.
- Economy Standard is SkyItalia's most popular fare type, offering the best balance of flexibility and baggage allowance for most travelers.

### 4.3 Economy Flex

- **1 checked bag included** (up to **23 kg**).
- Maximum linear dimensions: 158 cm.
- **Additional bag:** EUR 25 online, EUR 40 at airport.
- Economy Flex offers the most generous Economy-class terms, with lower fees for additional baggage and greater change/cancellation flexibility.

### 4.4 Premium Economy

- **1 checked bag included** (up to **23 kg**).
- **Second bag: free** (up to **23 kg**).
- Maximum linear dimensions per bag: 158 cm.
- Premium Economy passengers benefit from priority baggage handling, with a "PRIORITY" tag applied to all checked bags to ensure they are among the first to appear on the carousel at the destination.

### 4.5 Business Class

- **2 checked bags included** (up to **32 kg each**).
- Maximum linear dimensions per bag: 158 cm.
- **Third bag:** EUR 50.
- Business Class passengers benefit from priority baggage handling and SkyItalia's "First on Belt" service, where Business Class bags are loaded in the last ULD to ensure they are the first unloaded at the destination.

## 6. Excess, Overweight, and Oversized Baggage

### 6.1 Fee Schedule

| Condition | Fee |
|---|---|
| Overweight bag (23–32 kg) | EUR 50 per bag |
| Oversized bag (exceeds 158 cm linear dimensions) | EUR 75 per bag |
| Both overweight AND oversized (same bag) | EUR 100 per bag (combined) |
| Extra weight above 32 kg | **Not accepted** — must be split into two bags |
| Each additional bag beyond allowance | EUR 60 online, EUR 80 at airport |

### 6.2 Maximum Limits

- **Maximum weight per single piece:** 32 kg. No single bag may exceed 32 kg under any circumstance. Bags presented at check-in weighing more than 32 kg must be repacked or split. This restriction is mandated by IATA ground handling safety regulations and is non-negotiable.
- **Maximum linear dimensions per single piece:** 203 cm (length + width + height). Bags exceeding 203 cm cannot be transported as checked baggage and must be shipped as cargo through SkyItalia Cargo (separate service, separate booking required).
- **Maximum total checked baggage per passenger:** 5 pieces (regardless of fare type, loyalty status, and purchased additional bags combined). Passengers requiring more than 5 checked bags must contact SkyItalia Special Services at least 72 hours before departure.

### 6.3 Overweight Bag Processing

When a passenger presents an overweight bag at check-in:
1. The check-in agent weighs the bag and identifies the excess.
2. The agent informs the passenger of the overweight fee (EUR 50 per bag for bags between 23 and 32 kg).
3. The passenger may choose to: (a) pay the fee, (b) repack items into another bag or carry-on, or (c) remove items.
4. Payment is processed at the check-in counter (credit/debit card only; cash not accepted at most SkyItalia counters).
5. An overweight tag (orange) is applied to the bag for ground handling identification.

### 6.4 Oversized Bag Processing

Oversized bags (exceeding 158 cm but within 203 cm) are accepted as checked baggage with the EUR 75 surcharge. These bags are transported in the bulk cargo hold of the aircraft and may not be placed in standard ULDs. On smaller aircraft (Boeing 737-800), oversized bag capacity is limited to 4 items per flight on a first-come, first-served basis.

## 7. SkyMiles Loyalty Program — Baggage Benefits

SkyItalia's loyalty program, SkyMiles, provides enhanced baggage allowances to its members based on their tier status. These benefits are applied automatically when the SkyMiles member ID is associated with the booking.

### 7.1 Tier-Based Baggage Benefits

| Tier | Additional Free Checked Bags | Weight Allowance | Priority Handling | Other |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Blue (Base) | 0 | Standard fare allowance | No | Earn points on checked bag purchases |
| Silver | 0 | Standard fare allowance | No | Priority waitlist for overbooked flights |
| **Gold** | **+1 additional free checked bag (23 kg)** | 23 kg per additional bag | **Yes** | Priority check-in counter, priority baggage handling |
| **Platinum** | **+2 additional free checked bags (32 kg each)** | 32 kg per additional bag | **Yes** | Lounge access at all partner lounges worldwide, First on Belt service |

### 7.2 How Loyalty Baggage Benefits Stack

Loyalty baggage benefits are additive to the fare-type allowance. Examples:

- **Gold member on Economy Light:** 0 (fare) + 1 (Gold) = **1 checked bag at 23 kg**, free of charge.
- **Gold member on Economy Standard:** 1 (fare) + 1 (Gold) = **2 checked bags at 23 kg each**, free of charge.
- **Gold member on Business:** 2 (fare) + 1 (Gold) = **3 checked bags** (2 at 32 kg from Business, 1 at 23 kg from Gold).
- **Platinum member on Economy Light:** 0 (fare) + 2 (Platinum) = **2 checked bags at 32 kg each**, free of charge.
- **Platinum member on Economy Standard:** 1 (fare at 23 kg) + 2 (Platinum at 32 kg) = **3 checked bags**.
- **Platinum member on Business:** 2 (fare) + 2 (Platinum) = **4 checked bags at 32 kg each**, free of charge.

### 7.3 Important Notes on Loyalty Baggage

- Loyalty benefits apply only when the SkyMiles member ID is correctly entered in the booking at the time of check-in.
- If the member ID is added after check-in, the benefits cannot be applied retroactively for that flight. Points will still be credited, but the extra baggage allowance will not be honored.
- Loyalty baggage benefits are personal and non-transferable. They apply only to the member's own booking, not to companions traveling on separate bookings.
- SkyMiles members traveling on award tickets (booked using points) receive the same baggage benefits as their tier level.
- If a loyalty member's bag is lost or delayed, their claim receives priority processing (Gold: within 24 hours; Platinum: within 12 hours).

### 7.4 SkyMiles Partner Card — Additional Benefit

Holders of the SkyItalia Visa Partner Card (issued by Banca Italiana) receive an additional checked bag allowance of 1 bag up to 23 kg on all SkyItalia-operated flights, regardless of SkyMiles tier. This benefit stacks with both fare-type and tier-based allowances, up to the maximum of 5 pieces per passenger. The Partner Card must be used to pay for the ticket to activate this benefit.

## 8. Special Items — Musical Instruments

### 8.1 General Policy

SkyItalia welcomes passengers traveling with musical instruments. The transport of instruments is governed by specific rules depending on the instrument's size, weight, and fragility.

### 8.2 Small Instruments — Cabin Transport

Small instruments may be brought as cabin baggage if they fit in the overhead bin or under the seat. They count as the passenger's carry-on bag allowance.

| Instrument | Typical Dimensions (case included) | Cabin Eligible? | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Violin / Viola | 80 x 25 x 14 cm | Yes | Fits overhead bin on all SkyItalia aircraft |
| Flute | 45 x 12 x 8 cm | Yes | Fits under seat |
| Clarinet | 70 x 14 x 10 cm | Yes | Fits overhead bin |
| Trumpet (in case) | 55 x 30 x 18 cm | Yes | Just within carry-on limits |
| Acoustic Guitar (in soft case) | 105 x 40 x 14 cm | Requires extra seat | Exceeds 55 cm length limit |
| Electric Guitar (in hard case) | 110 x 42 x 16 cm | Requires extra seat | Exceeds carry-on limits |
| Ukulele | 70 x 28 x 12 cm | Yes | Fits overhead bin |
| French Horn (in case) | 55 x 55 x 35 cm | Requires extra seat | Exceeds width and depth limits |

### 8.3 Extra Seat for Instruments

When an instrument cannot fit in the overhead bin or under the seat, the passenger must purchase an extra seat for the instrument.

- **Price for extra seat:** **50% of the ticket price** paid by the passenger for the same fare type and route.
- The instrument must be secured in its case and placed on the purchased seat with the seatbelt fastened around it.
- The extra seat is booked by contacting SkyItalia Customer Service (cannot be booked online). The seat is blocked as "CBBG" (Cabin Baggage) in the reservation system.
- No baggage fees apply for the instrument when transported on a purchased extra seat.

### 8.4 Large Instruments — Checked or Cargo

Large instruments that cannot be accommodated in the cabin, even on an extra seat, must be transported as checked baggage or via SkyItalia Cargo.

| Instrument | Typical Dimensions (case included) | Transport Method |
|---|---|---|
| Cello (in hard case) | 135 x 50 x 35 cm | Extra seat (mandatory) |
| Double Bass (in flight case) | 200 x 65 x 40 cm | SkyItalia Cargo only |
| Harp (concert, in case) | 190 x 100 x 55 cm | SkyItalia Cargo only |
| Tuba (in case) | 90 x 60 x 60 cm | Checked baggage (oversize fee applies) or extra seat |
| Drum Kit (multiple cases) | Varies | SkyItalia Cargo recommended |
| Piano / Keyboard (88 keys, in case) | 150 x 45 x 20 cm | Checked baggage (oversize fee applies) |

For instruments transported as checked baggage, standard overweight and oversized fees apply. SkyItalia accepts no additional liability for damage to musical instruments transported as checked baggage beyond the standard Montreal Convention limits. Passengers are strongly recommended to use hard-shell flight cases and to purchase supplemental baggage insurance.

### 8.5 Professional Musicians — Special Arrangements

Professional musicians traveling with multiple instruments or a full ensemble should contact SkyItalia Special Services at least 7 business days before departure. SkyItalia can arrange dedicated cargo space, ULD booking, and special handling procedures. A dedicated quote will be provided. Orchestras with 20+ instruments traveling together may qualify for a group instrument rate (approximately 20% discount on standard cargo rates — quote required).

E-bikes are subject to additional restrictions due to the lithium battery:
- The battery must be removed from the bicycle and carried in the cabin.
- The battery must not exceed 160 Wh. Batteries over 160 Wh are not permitted on SkyItalia flights.
- The battery terminals must be protected against short circuit (tape over terminals or in a protective pouch).
- The bicycle frame (without battery) follows standard bicycle transport rules (EUR 60, boxed/bagged, handlebars turned, pedals removed).

## 10. Special Items — Mobility and Medical Equipment

### 10.1 Wheelchairs and Mobility Aids

Passengers with reduced mobility are entitled to transport their wheelchair or mobility aid **free of charge** on all SkyItalia flights, in addition to their standard baggage allowance. This includes:

- Manual wheelchairs (folding and rigid-frame)
- Electric wheelchairs (battery-powered)
- Mobility scooters
- Walking frames and rollators
- Crutches and canes

Electric wheelchairs with lithium-ion batteries: the battery must not exceed 300 Wh (or two batteries not exceeding 160 Wh each). For wet-cell (spillable) batteries, the battery must be disconnected, terminals insulated, and the battery securely attached to the wheelchair. Passengers must notify SkyItalia at least 48 hours before departure if traveling with an electric wheelchair.

### 10.2 Medical Equipment

The following medical equipment is transported free of charge and does not count toward the baggage allowance:
- CPAP machines (may be used on board with battery power)
- Portable oxygen concentrators (FAA/EASA approved models only — must notify SkyItalia 72 hours in advance)
- Dialysis equipment
- Medication and medical supplies in reasonable quantities

## 11. Animals in the Hold

### 11.1 General Hold Transport Policy

Animals that are too large to travel in the cabin (see FAQ document for cabin pet policy) may be transported in the temperature-controlled, pressurized cargo hold of SkyItalia aircraft.

**Note:** This section covers hold transport only. For cabin pet transport (small dogs and cats in carrier under the seat), refer to the FAQ document Section on Animals.

### 11.2 Eligible Animals

- Dogs of any breed and size (except breeds restricted by destination country legislation)
- Cats
- Rabbits and ferrets (select routes only)
- Other small mammals — by prior arrangement with SkyItalia Special Services

**Not eligible for hold transport:** Brachycephalic (snub-nosed) dog breeds and cat breeds during the months of June, July, and August (temperature risk). This includes but is not limited to: Bulldogs (English, French, American), Pugs, Boston Terriers, Shih Tzus, Boxers, Pekingese, Persian cats, Himalayan cats, and Exotic Shorthairs. This restriction is in effect from 1 June through 31 August regardless of the destination temperature.

### 11.3 Fees and Weight Limits for Hold Animals

| Weight Category (animal + crate) | Fee per Segment |
|---|---|
| Up to 23 kg | EUR 100 |
| 23–50 kg | EUR 200 |
| 50–75 kg | EUR 300 |
| Over 75 kg | SkyItalia Cargo only |

### 11.4 Crate Requirements

- IATA-compliant rigid kennel/crate
- Large enough for the animal to stand, turn around, and lie down comfortably
- Ventilation on at least 3 sides
- Leak-proof bottom with absorbent material
- Secure locking mechanism (cable ties are not sufficient)
- Food and water containers attached inside (accessible from outside for ground staff)
- "LIVE ANIMAL" sticker on top and two sides
- Passenger's name, flight number, and contact number on the crate

### 11.5 Booking and Documentation

Hold animal transport must be booked at least 72 hours before departure by calling SkyItalia Customer Service. Maximum 2 animals per flight in the hold (capacity limit). Required documentation:
- EU Pet Passport or equivalent veterinary certificate
- Rabies vaccination (administered at least 21 days before travel)
- Health certificate issued within 10 days of travel
- Microchip number matching passport/certificate
- Import permits for destination country (if applicable)

## 12. Infant and Child Baggage

### 12.1 Infants (Under 2 Years)

Infants under 2 years traveling on a parent's or guardian's lap (no separate seat purchased) are entitled to:

- **1 checked bag** up to **10 kg** (max 115 cm linear dimensions), **free of charge**
- **1 fully collapsible stroller** OR **1 car seat**, **free of charge** (gate-checked or check-in counter)
- No carry-on bag allowance for the infant (the adult's allowance applies)

If an infant has a separate seat purchased (at child fare), they receive the full baggage allowance of the fare type booked.

### 12.2 Children (2–11 Years)

Children aged 2–11 holding a ticket (regardless of whether a child fare discount was applied) receive the **same baggage allowance** as an adult passenger on the same fare type.

### 12.3 Unaccompanied Minors

Unaccompanied minors (aged 5–17) receive the standard baggage allowance for their fare type. The unaccompanied minor service fee does not include any baggage benefit. See the FAQ document for unaccompanied minor procedures and fees.

## 13. Group Travel Policies (10+ Passengers)

### 13.1 Definition

A group booking is defined as a reservation for 10 or more passengers traveling on the same flight(s) under the same group booking reference. Group bookings are managed by the SkyItalia Group Desk and are subject to specific terms.

### 13.2 Group Baggage Allowance

Group passengers receive the standard baggage allowance for their fare type. However, groups benefit from the following special provisions:

- **Baggage pooling:** The total checked baggage weight allowance of the group is pooled. For example, a group of 10 passengers on Economy Standard has a pooled allowance of 10 x 23 kg = 230 kg. Individual bags may exceed 23 kg as long as: (a) no single bag exceeds 32 kg, and (b) the total group weight does not exceed the pooled allowance.
- **Reduced excess baggage fee:** EUR 45 per additional bag (online) instead of the standard EUR 60, provided the group booking reference is used.
- **Equipment pooling for sports groups:** Sports teams traveling with team equipment may consolidate equipment into fewer, larger bags. The same total weight and piece limits apply, but SkyItalia Ground Handling will arrange special handling for consolidated sports team bags.

### 13.3 Group Leader

Every group must designate a Group Leader who is the single point of contact for all baggage inquiries. The Group Leader is responsible for communicating baggage policies to all group members and for coordinating any special baggage requests (instruments, sports equipment, mobility aids) at least 7 days before departure.

### 13.4 School and Youth Groups

School groups (students under 18 accompanied by teachers/chaperones) traveling on SkyItalia receive a 15% discount on all sports equipment fees. This discount is applied automatically when the group is booked through the SkyItalia Education Desk.

## 14. Military and Diplomatic Exceptions

### 14.1 Active Military Personnel

Active-duty military personnel of NATO member states traveling on official orders (with military ID and travel authorization) are entitled to:

- **2 checked bags up to 32 kg each**, free of charge, on all fare types (including Economy Light)
- **1 additional bag** for military equipment (uniform bag, helmet, etc.) up to 23 kg, free of charge
- Overweight waiver: bags up to 32 kg are accepted without the overweight surcharge
- Firearms and ammunition are NOT covered by this exception and are subject to separate dangerous goods regulations and prior approval (see Section 19)

### 14.2 Diplomatic Personnel

Accredited diplomats traveling on official duty (with diplomatic passport and note verbale from the sending state's Ministry of Foreign Affairs) are entitled to:

- **2 checked bags up to 32 kg each**, free of charge, on all fare types
- **Diplomatic pouch:** accepted as additional cabin baggage item, not subject to screening (in accordance with the Vienna Convention on Diplomatic Relations, Article 27). Maximum 1 pouch per diplomat. Must be clearly marked as diplomatic mail.
- Excess baggage fees are waived for up to 2 additional bags per diplomat traveling on official duty.

### 14.3 Documentation

All military and diplomatic exceptions must be verified at the check-in counter. Self-check-in kiosks and online check-in do not apply these exceptions automatically. Personnel should arrive at the check-in counter at least 90 minutes before departure to allow for processing.

## 15. Codeshare and Interline Baggage Rules

### 15.1 Most-Significant-Carrier Rule

When a passenger travels on an itinerary involving both SkyItalia and one or more other carriers (interline or codeshare), the baggage policy of the "most significant carrier" (MSC) applies for the entire itinerary. The MSC is determined according to IATA Resolution 302:

1. On a one-way itinerary: the first international carrier (crossing an international boundary). If all sectors are domestic, the first carrier.
2. On a round-trip itinerary: the carrier on the first international sector from the point of origin.

### 15.2 SkyAlliance Partner Carriers

SkyItalia is a member of the SkyAlliance partnership. Fellow SkyAlliance members include: AlpineAir (Switzerland), NordWing (Scandinavia), AtlantisJet (Portugal), SolAir (Spain), and HellenicSky (Greece). When the MSC is a SkyAlliance partner, the partner's baggage policy applies. However, SkyMiles loyalty tier benefits (Gold +1 bag, Platinum +2 bags) are honored on all SkyAlliance operated flights provided the SkyMiles number is in the booking.

### 15.3 Non-Alliance Codeshare

For flights operated by non-alliance carriers under a SkyItalia codeshare (AZ flight number but operated by another airline), the operating carrier's baggage policy applies. Passengers should check the operating carrier's policy at the time of booking. SkyItalia customer service agents can provide operating carrier baggage information upon request.

### 15.4 Connection Baggage Handling

When connecting between SkyItalia and a partner carrier at an Italian hub (MXP, FCO, LIN), baggage is through-checked to the final destination provided:
- The itinerary is on a single ticket (same PNR or interline PNR)
- The minimum connection time is met (90 minutes domestic, 120 minutes international at MXP; 75 minutes domestic, 105 minutes international at FCO)
- The baggage complies with both carriers' policies

If the itineraries are on separate tickets, passengers must collect their baggage, clear customs (if applicable), and re-check at the connecting carrier's check-in counter.

## 16. Strollers, Car Seats, and Child Equipment

### 16.1 Free Transport

The following items are transported **free of charge** for passengers traveling with infants or children (under 12 years):

- **1 stroller** (collapsible umbrella stroller, full-size stroller, or jogging stroller): gate-checked or checked at counter
- **1 car seat** (child restraint system): may be used on board if the child has a purchased seat and the car seat is EASA-approved, or checked
- **1 travel cot / portable bassinet**: checked at counter

These items do not count toward the checked baggage allowance and are transported in addition to the passenger's standard allowance.

### 16.2 Gate Check for Strollers

Strollers may be used up to the aircraft door. SkyItalia ground staff will gate-check the stroller and return it at the aircraft door upon arrival. If the destination airport does not support aircraft-door delivery (certain remote stands or bus-boarding situations), the stroller will be delivered to the baggage carousel.

Double strollers (twin/tandem) and strollers exceeding 15 kg empty weight may need to be checked at the counter due to aircraft hold space limitations on smaller aircraft (Boeing 737-800). SkyItalia recommends checking oversized strollers at the counter.

## 17. Prohibited Items — Cabin Baggage

The following items are **not permitted in the aircraft cabin** and will be confiscated at the security checkpoint or boarding gate:

- **Liquids, aerosols, gels (LAGs)** over 100 ml per container (unless purchased after security at the airport or on board)
- All LAGs must be in containers of 100 ml or less, placed in a single transparent, resealable plastic bag (maximum 1 liter capacity)
- **Sharp objects:** knives of any kind, scissors with blades over 6 cm, razor blades (safety razors with cartridge are permitted), box cutters, letter openers, corkscrews with blade
- **Blunt instruments:** baseball bats, cricket bats, golf clubs, martial arts equipment, billiard cues
- **Tools:** drills, saws, screwdrivers over 6 cm, wrenches over 6 cm, crowbars, hammers
- **Stunning/incapacitating devices:** tasers, pepper spray, mace, stun guns
- **Lighters:** maximum 1 disposable lighter per person, carried in pocket only (not in cabin baggage). Torch/jet lighters and lighter fuel are prohibited in all baggage.
- **Toy weapons / replicas** that could be confused with real weapons

## 18. Prohibited Items — All Baggage (Cabin and Checked)

The following items are prohibited in both cabin and checked baggage on all SkyItalia flights:

- **Explosives and incendiary devices:** fireworks, flares, signal flares, detonators, fuses, mines, grenades, ammunition (except as specifically authorized — see Section 19)
- **Flammable liquids and gases:** petrol, lighter fluid, paint thinner, acetone in large quantities, propane, butane, aerosol cans with flammable propellant over 500 ml
- **Toxic and infectious substances:** rat poison, insecticide concentrate, biological cultures, virus samples, medical waste
- **Corrosive substances:** wet-cell batteries (except wheelchair batteries under Section 10), mercury, strong acids (e.g., sulfuric, hydrochloric), strong alkalis (e.g., lye, caustic soda)
- **Radioactive materials** (any amount, without exception)
- **Magnetic materials** exceeding 0.002 T at 2.1 m from the outside of the package
- **Oxidizing materials:** bleach, peroxide over 8% concentration, pool chemicals
- **Lithium batteries over 160 Wh** (lithium batteries under 100 Wh may be in cabin only; batteries between 100–160 Wh may be in cabin with airline approval, max 2 spare batteries per passenger)

## 19. Hazardous Materials — Extended Reference List

This section provides a comprehensive (but not exhaustive) reference list of commonly encountered hazardous materials and their transport restrictions. This list supplements the prohibitions in Sections 17 and 18.

### 19.1 Lithium Batteries — Detailed Rules

| Battery Type | Watt-hours (Wh) | Cabin? | Checked? | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Contained in device (phone, laptop, camera) | Under 100 Wh | Yes | Yes | No limit on number |
| Contained in device | 100–160 Wh | Yes (with approval) | Yes (with approval) | Max 2 devices; notify at check-in |
| Contained in device | Over 160 Wh | No | No | Ship via cargo |
| Spare/loose battery | Under 100 Wh | Yes | **No** | Terminals must be insulated; max 20 spare batteries |
| Spare/loose battery | 100–160 Wh | Yes (with approval) | **No** | Max 2 spare batteries; terminals insulated |
| Spare/loose battery | Over 160 Wh | No | No | Ship via cargo |
| Power bank (portable charger) | Under 100 Wh | Yes | **No** | Same rules as spare battery |
| Power bank | 100–160 Wh | Yes (with approval) | **No** | Max 2; notify at check-in |
| E-cigarette / vape battery | Under 100 Wh | Yes | **No** | Must be in cabin; not permitted in checked baggage |

**How to calculate Wh:** Wh = Voltage (V) x Capacity (Ah). Example: a 3.7V, 5000mAh power bank = 3.7 x 5.0 = 18.5 Wh.

### 19.2 Firearms and Ammunition (Authorized Transport)

Transport of sporting firearms is permitted on SkyItalia flights under the following conditions:
- Declared at check-in; firearms declaration form completed
- Unloaded; in a hard-sided, locked container (only the passenger has the key/combination)
- Ammunition: in original manufacturer's packaging or securely boxed; max 5 kg per passenger; in checked baggage only
- Separate from the firearm in the checked baggage
- Must comply with Italian firearms legislation and destination country regulations
- Advance notification: at least 72 hours before departure via SkyItalia Special Services
- Fee: EUR 50 per firearm container per direction (in addition to standard checked baggage fees)

### 19.3 Dry Ice

- Maximum 2.5 kg per passenger in checked baggage
- Must be for preserving perishable items (e.g., medical samples)
- The container must allow CO2 gas to release (not hermetically sealed)
- Package must be marked "DRY ICE" or "CARBON DIOXIDE, SOLID" with net weight
- Notification at check-in required

### 19.4 Self-Heating Meals and Chemical Heat Packs

- Permitted in cabin baggage in quantities for personal use
- Not permitted in checked baggage (risk of accidental activation in unpressurized areas)
- Maximum 5 individual self-heating meal packs per passenger

### 19.5 Electronic Cigarettes and Vaping Devices

- **Cabin only** — never in checked baggage
- Must be switched off during the flight
- Charging on board is not permitted
- Refillable liquid containers must comply with LAGs rules (100 ml max, in clear bag)
- Use of e-cigarettes/vapes is prohibited on all SkyItalia flights, including in lavatories

## 21. Lost, Delayed, or Damaged Baggage — Procedures and Timelines

### 21.1 Immediate Steps at the Airport

If your checked baggage does not appear on the carousel at your destination:

1. **Do not leave the baggage claim area** without first reporting the issue.
2. Proceed to the **Baggage Service Desk** (BSD), located in the arrivals hall of every airport served by SkyItalia.
3. A BSD agent will search the baggage tracking system (WorldTracer) for your bag using your baggage tag receipt number (affixed to your boarding pass or provided at check-in).
4. The agent will file a **PIR (Property Irregularity Report)**. You will receive a PIR reference number (format: MXPAZ12345). Keep this number safe — you will need it for all follow-up communications.
5. Provide the agent with your local contact details (address, phone, email) for delivery once the bag is located.

### 21.2 Timeline for Delayed Baggage

| Timeframe | SkyItalia Action |
|---|---|
| **0–6 hours** | Automated WorldTracer search initiated. SMS/email updates to passenger. |
| **6–24 hours** | Dedicated agent assigned to trace. Passenger may purchase essential items (see reimbursement below). |
| **24–48 hours** | Escalated trace involving origin and transit airports. SkyItalia contacts partner carriers if applicable. Gold/Platinum SkyMiles members receive priority outreach from Customer Relations. |
| **48 hours – 5 days** | Extended network search. SkyItalia proactively contacts the passenger every 24 hours with updates. If the bag is located, it is routed on the next available flight and delivered to the passenger's address. |
| **5–21 days** | Continued search. Passenger may submit interim expense claims for essential items purchased during the delay. |
| **After 21 days** | If the bag has not been found within 21 days of the PIR filing, it is officially classified as **lost**. The passenger is entitled to file a formal claim for compensation. |

### 21.3 Essential Items Reimbursement (Delayed Baggage)

If your baggage is delayed and you need to purchase essential items (toiletries, basic clothing, medication), SkyItalia will reimburse reasonable expenses:

- **Domestic flights:** up to EUR 50 per day, maximum EUR 150 total
- **International flights:** up to EUR 100 per day, maximum EUR 350 total
- Original receipts required for all purchases
- Non-essential items (electronics, luxury goods, souvenirs) are not eligible for reimbursement
- Claims must be submitted within 21 days of the baggage being returned or declared lost

### 21.4 Damaged Baggage

If your checked baggage arrives damaged:

1. Report the damage immediately at the **Baggage Service Desk** before leaving the baggage claim area.
2. The agent will photograph the damage and file a PIR.
3. SkyItalia will either repair the bag or offer a replacement bag of equivalent value, or provide monetary compensation.
4. If not reported at the airport: you must file a written claim within **7 days** of receiving the damaged baggage via the SkyItalia website (skyitalia.com/claims) or by registered mail.
5. Claims filed after 7 days will not be accepted.

### 21.5 Lost Baggage — Formal Claim

If your bag is declared lost (21 days without recovery):

1. SkyItalia Customer Relations will contact you to initiate the formal compensation process.
2. You will be asked to provide: (a) a detailed inventory of the bag's contents with estimated values, (b) purchase receipts for high-value items (if available), (c) the original PIR reference number.
3. SkyItalia will evaluate the claim and offer compensation within **30 business days** of receiving the complete documentation.
4. Maximum liability under the **Montreal Convention**: approximately **EUR 1,400** per passenger (approximately 1,288 Special Drawing Rights as per the current conversion rate). This limit applies regardless of the actual value of the lost items.
5. Passengers who purchased SkyItalia Baggage Protection insurance at the time of booking (EUR 8 per passenger, per direction) are covered for up to EUR 3,000 per bag, with a simplified claims process and payout within 10 business days.

### 21.6 Compensation for Contents — Depreciation

When calculating compensation for the contents of a lost bag, SkyItalia applies a depreciation model:
- Items less than 6 months old: 100% of purchase price (with receipt)
- Items 6–12 months old: 80% of purchase price
- Items 1–3 years old: 50% of purchase price
- Items over 3 years old: 30% of purchase price
- Items without a receipt: standard flat-rate value based on item category (e.g., shirt: EUR 20, trousers: EUR 35, shoes: EUR 50, laptop: EUR 400)

### 21.7 Filing a Claim Online

All baggage claims (delay, damage, loss) can be filed via the SkyItalia website:
1. Go to skyitalia.com/claims
2. Select "Baggage Claim"
3. Enter your PIR reference number, booking reference, and flight details
4. Upload supporting documentation (receipts, photos of damage, inventory list)
5. Submit the claim
6. You will receive an automated confirmation email with a claim reference number (format: CLM-2026-XXXXX)
7. SkyItalia aims to respond to all baggage claims within 30 business days

## 22. Baggage Tagging, Tracking, and Identification

### 22.1 Baggage Tags

At check-in (counter or self-service kiosk), a baggage tag is printed and attached to each checked bag. The tag contains:
- A 10-digit license plate number (barcode and human-readable)
- The routing (origin airport code → destination airport code, with any connections)
- The passenger's name
- The flight number and date

Passengers must keep the baggage tag receipt (the smaller portion detached at check-in) until their bags are collected at the destination. The receipt is essential for filing any baggage irregularity report.

### 22.2 SkyItalia Baggage Tracking App

SkyItalia offers real-time baggage tracking through the SkyItalia mobile app. After check-in, passengers can view:
- The status of their checked baggage (checked in, loaded, in transit, arrived, on carousel)
- Estimated time of appearance on the carousel
- Push notifications for delays or routing issues

To use the tracking feature, passengers must have the SkyItalia app installed and their booking reference linked to their account. The tracking feature works on all SkyItalia-operated flights and on SkyAlliance partner flights from participating airports.

### 22.3 Identification of Unclaimed Baggage

Bags that remain unclaimed on the carousel after 24 hours are moved to the airport's Lost & Found storage. After 5 days, if not claimed, the bag is transferred to SkyItalia's central baggage storage facility in Milan. After 90 days, unclaimed bags are handled according to Italian civil aviation regulations (CAA-IT Regulation 327/2018), which may include disposal or auction of contents.

Passengers are strongly recommended to attach an external luggage tag with their name, phone number, and email address to every checked bag, in addition to the airline-issued tag.

## 23. Connecting Flights and Through-Check Baggage

### 23.1 Through-Check on SkyItalia Connections

When a passenger has a connecting flight on SkyItalia (two or more SkyItalia-operated flights on the same ticket), checked baggage is automatically through-checked to the final destination. The passenger does not need to collect and re-check bags at intermediate stops.

**Exception:** If the connection involves clearing customs at an EU border (e.g., arriving from a non-EU country into an Italian hub and then connecting to a domestic flight), the passenger must collect their baggage, clear customs, and re-check the baggage. SkyItalia provides a dedicated "Connection Re-Check" counter at MXP Terminal 1 and FCO Terminal 3 for this purpose.

### 23.2 Minimum Connection Times

| Airport | Domestic → Domestic | Domestic → International | International → Domestic | International → International |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| MXP (Malpensa) | 60 min | 90 min | 120 min | 90 min |
| FCO (Fiumicino) | 50 min | 75 min | 105 min | 75 min |
| LIN (Linate) | 45 min | N/A | N/A | N/A |

If a passenger misses a connection due to a delay on the first SkyItalia flight, SkyItalia will rebook the passenger and ensure their baggage is re-routed to the new flights at no additional cost.

### 23.3 Self-Transfer (Separate Tickets)

If a passenger has booked separate tickets (e.g., one SkyItalia ticket and one ticket on another carrier), baggage is NOT through-checked. The passenger must collect their baggage at the first destination, proceed through arrivals, re-enter the departure area, check in for the second flight, and re-check their baggage. All applicable baggage fees for the second ticket apply independently.

## 24. Seasonal Policies and Peak Travel Adjustments

### 24.1 Peak Travel Periods

SkyItalia defines the following as peak travel periods:
- **Christmas/New Year:** 18 December – 6 January
- **Easter:** Thursday before Easter – Tuesday after Easter Monday
- **Summer:** 15 June – 15 September
- **Italian Public Holidays:** 25 April (Liberation Day), 1 May (Labour Day), 2 June (Republic Day), 15 August (Ferragosto)

During peak periods, cabin baggage enforcement is stricter than usual. Passengers are strongly encouraged to check baggage rather than rely on overhead bin space, which fills quickly on high-load-factor flights.

### 24.2 Winter Sports Season

From 15 December to 15 March, SkyItalia operates additional flights on routes serving ski destinations (Turin, Innsbruck, Geneva, Salzburg, Chambery). During this period:
- Ski and snowboard equipment booking is strongly recommended at least 48 hours in advance
- A maximum of 30 ski/snowboard sets per flight applies on Boeing 737-800 aircraft (40 sets on A320neo/A321neo)
- If the limit is reached, late-booking passengers will be offered transport on the next available flight or via SkyItalia Cargo

### 24.3 Summer Surf and Dive Season

From 1 June to 30 September, SkyItalia sees increased demand for surfboard and diving equipment transport, particularly on routes to Sardinia, Sicily, and the Canary Islands (via codeshare). Passengers are advised to book sports equipment early. Surfboard capacity is limited to 10 boards per flight on Boeing 737-800 (15 on A320neo/A321neo).
