# SkyItalia Airlines — Frequently Asked Questions

This document contains comprehensive answers to the most common questions our passengers ask. For policy-specific details, please also refer to our dedicated policy documents: Baggage Policy, Booking Change & Cancellation Policy, Compensation Policy, and SkyMiles Loyalty Program guide.

## 1. Check-in

### 1.1 Online Check-in

**When can I check in online?**
Online check-in opens **48 hours** before departure and closes **1 hour** before departure. You can check in at skyitalia.com or via the SkyItalia mobile app.

**How do I check in online step by step?**
1. Go to skyitalia.com/checkin or open the SkyItalia app.
2. Enter your booking reference (6-character alphanumeric code found in your confirmation email) and your last name.
3. Confirm your personal details and travel documents (passport or ID number).
4. Select or confirm your seat assignment. If you have not pre-selected a seat, you will be shown available options at this stage.
5. Add any ancillary services you may need (extra baggage, meals, Wi-Fi packages).
6. Review the summary and confirm.
7. Download your boarding pass as a PDF or save it to your mobile wallet (Apple Wallet or Google Wallet).

**Can I check in online if I'm traveling with an infant?**
Yes. When checking in online, you must indicate that you are traveling with a lap infant. The system will prompt you to enter the infant's full name, date of birth, and travel document number. Note that infants do not receive a separate boarding pass; the infant notation will appear on the accompanying adult's boarding pass. If you are unable to complete the infant check-in online for any reason, you may finalize the process at the airport check-in counter without incurring additional fees.

**Can I check in online for a group booking?**
For groups of up to 9 passengers on the same booking, online check-in is available. Each passenger in the group can be checked in during the same session. For groups of 10 or more, online check-in is not available; please use the dedicated group check-in counter at the airport. Group bookings managed through a travel agency may have different check-in procedures — contact your travel agent for details.

**I completed online check-in but forgot to download my boarding pass. What should I do?**
You can retrieve your boarding pass at any time before departure by logging into your booking on skyitalia.com or the SkyItalia app. Alternatively, you may print your boarding pass at an airport self-service kiosk or request a reprint at the check-in counter.

### 1.2 Mobile App Check-in

**Can I use the SkyItalia app to check in?**
Yes. The SkyItalia app (available on iOS and Android) offers the same check-in functionality as the website. The app also stores your boarding pass digitally and sends real-time notifications about gate changes, boarding times, and delays.

**Does the app work offline after check-in?**
Your boarding pass is stored locally on your device after check-in, so it remains accessible even without an internet connection. However, real-time updates (gate changes, delays) require an active connection. We recommend enabling push notifications for the most up-to-date information.

**Can I add my boarding pass to Apple Wallet or Google Wallet?**
Yes. After completing check-in, you will be prompted to add your boarding pass to your mobile wallet. This works for both individual and group bookings (one pass per passenger).

### 1.3 Airport Kiosk Check-in

**How do I use the self-service kiosks at the airport?**
SkyItalia self-service kiosks are available at all airports we serve. To use one:
1. Scan your passport, national ID card, or booking confirmation barcode.
2. Follow the on-screen prompts to confirm your identity and booking details.
3. Select or change your seat if desired (fees may apply depending on your fare type).
4. Print your boarding pass and any baggage tags.
5. If you have checked baggage, proceed to the bag drop counter with your printed tags.

Kiosks are typically available from **3 hours** before departure. Languages available on kiosks include Italian, English, French, German, Spanish, and Portuguese.

### 1.4 Airport Counter Check-in

**When does airport check-in open?**
Airport check-in counters open **3 hours** before departure and close **45 minutes** before departure. For Business Class passengers, counters close **30 minutes** before departure.

**Can I check in at the airport without doing it online first?**
Yes. However, online check-in is recommended as some fare types (Economy Light) may charge a fee of **EUR 10** for airport-only check-in.

**Is there a dedicated check-in counter for Business Class?**
Yes. All airports served by SkyItalia have a dedicated Business Class check-in counter, which offers shorter queues and priority processing. Platinum SkyMiles members also have access to this counter regardless of their cabin class.

**Is there a priority check-in counter for loyalty members?**
Gold and Platinum SkyMiles members have access to the priority check-in counter at all airports. Silver members may use the priority counter at Milan Malpensa (MXP) and Rome Fiumicino (FCO) only, subject to availability.

### 1.5 Seat Changes at Check-in

**Can I change my seat at check-in?**
Yes. During online, kiosk, or counter check-in, you may change your seat assignment subject to availability. If you are moving to a seat category with a different price (e.g., from a standard seat to an extra-legroom seat), the fare difference applies. If you pre-paid for a specific seat and wish to change to a seat of equal or lesser value, no refund of the original seat fee is provided unless the change is due to an aircraft swap.

**What happens if I don't select a seat before check-in?**
For Economy Light fares, seats are assigned automatically at check-in. SkyItalia will assign you the best available seat at no charge, but we cannot guarantee window, aisle, or specific row preferences if you have not pre-selected. To guarantee a preferred seat, we recommend purchasing seat selection when booking.

### 1.7 Documents Required for Check-in

**What documents do I need to present at check-in?**
You need a valid government-issued photo ID: a national identity card (for flights within the EU/EEA/Switzerland) or a passport (for all other destinations). The name on your travel document must match the name on your booking exactly. For flights requiring a visa, you may also be asked to present your visa or electronic travel authorization at check-in.

**My name on the booking has a small typo. Will I be able to check in?**
Minor spelling corrections (e.g., one or two letters) can usually be handled at check-in or by contacting customer service in advance. However, the name on your booking should match your travel document as closely as possible. For significant name discrepancies, a name change fee may apply depending on your fare type (see our Change & Cancellation Policy). We strongly recommend verifying your name at the time of booking.

## 2. Documents and Travel Requirements

### 2.1 EU/EEA/Schengen Travel

**What documents do I need to fly within the EU?**
A valid national identity card or passport. All passengers, including infants, must have their own document.

**Can I use my driving license as ID for a flight within Italy?**
No. A driving license is not accepted as a travel document for air travel, even on domestic flights within Italy. You must present a valid national identity card (carta d'identita) or passport.

**Is a residence permit (permesso di soggiorno) accepted for travel?**
A residence permit alone is not a valid travel document for boarding a flight. Non-EU nationals holding a residence permit must present a valid passport. The residence permit may be required in addition to the passport for re-entry into Italy.

### 2.2 International Travel

**What documents do I need for flights outside the EU?**
A valid passport. Some destinations require a visa — please check entry requirements for your destination before traveling.

**How much validity must my passport have?**
For most international destinations, your passport must be valid for at least **6 months** beyond your planned date of return. Some countries require at least two blank pages for entry stamps. SkyItalia may deny boarding if your passport does not meet the entry requirements of your destination, as the airline can be fined by authorities for transporting passengers with insufficient documentation.

**Is my ID card enough for flights to the UK?**
No. Since Brexit, a valid **passport** is required for travel to/from the United Kingdom. National identity cards from EU member states are no longer accepted for travel to the UK as of October 2021, with limited exceptions for settled or pre-settled status holders under the EU Settlement Scheme.

### 2.3 Visa and ESTA Requirements

**Do I need a visa for travel to the United States?**
Italian citizens traveling to the United States for tourism or business (up to 90 days) do not need a traditional visa but must obtain an approved **ESTA (Electronic System for Travel Authorization)** before departure. The ESTA application should be submitted at least 72 hours before travel. ESTA costs USD 21 and is valid for two years or until your passport expires, whichever comes first. Citizens of other countries should check their eligibility for the Visa Waiver Program or apply for the appropriate visa.

**Does SkyItalia verify visa requirements at check-in?**
SkyItalia verifies that passengers hold the necessary travel documents at check-in. If you do not have the required visa or travel authorization for your destination, you may be denied boarding. SkyItalia is not responsible for verifying visa eligibility in advance; it is the passenger's responsibility to ensure they have all necessary documentation. We recommend using the IATA Travel Centre or your destination country's embassy website to check requirements.

**What documents are needed for transit passengers?**
Transit requirements vary by country. In some cases, even if you are only connecting through an airport and not entering the country, you may need a transit visa. For connections within the EU Schengen area, no additional documentation is required beyond your initial travel document. For transit through non-Schengen countries (e.g., the UK, Turkey, the US), check the specific transit visa requirements for that country.

### 2.4 Passports for Minors

**Do children and infants need their own passport?**
Yes. Every passenger, regardless of age, must have their own valid travel document. Children can no longer be included on a parent's passport for air travel. For international flights, each child (including infants) must have their own passport.

**What additional documents do children traveling with one parent need?**
While SkyItalia does not require additional documentation beyond a valid passport or ID card for minors traveling with one parent on EU flights, some destination countries may require a notarized authorization letter from the non-traveling parent. This is particularly common in countries such as Brazil, South Africa, and parts of Latin America. Check your destination's requirements well in advance.

### 2.5 Pet Travel Documents

**What documents does my pet need for air travel?**
For travel within the EU, pets require:
- A valid **EU Pet Passport** issued by an authorized veterinarian.
- Up-to-date **rabies vaccination** (at least 21 days before travel for first vaccination).
- A **microchip** (ISO 11784/11785 compliant).

For travel to the UK, additional requirements include a tapeworm treatment administered 1-5 days before travel (for dogs only). For travel outside the EU, requirements vary by destination; an international health certificate may be required, often issued within 10 days of travel. Some countries require an import permit or quarantine upon arrival.

**Where can I get an EU Pet Passport?**
Any veterinarian authorized by your national competent authority can issue an EU Pet Passport. In Italy, this is typically your local ASL (Azienda Sanitaria Locale) veterinary service. The passport must include your pet's microchip number, rabies vaccination history, and your contact details.

## 3. Children, Infants, and Unaccompanied Minors

### 3.1 Traveling with Infants (Under 2 Years)

**Do infants need a ticket?**
Infants under 2 years old travel on the lap of an adult for **EUR 25** per flight segment (no seat). Infants are not entitled to baggage allowance, but one stroller is carried free of charge.

**Can I bring a car seat on board for my infant?**
If you purchase a separate seat for your infant, you may bring an approved child restraint device (car seat) that meets ECE R44/04 or R129 certification. The car seat must be installed in a window seat and must not obstruct the aisle or the row's emergency exit access. Without a purchased seat, infants must be held on the adult's lap using the infant seatbelt extension provided by cabin crew during takeoff, landing, and turbulence.

**What items can I bring for my infant for free?**
In addition to the free stroller, you may bring one of the following items at no charge: a collapsible travel cot, a baby carrier (soft-structured or wrap), or a booster seat. These items are checked at the gate and returned upon arrival. Infant food, milk, and baby formula in reasonable quantities are exempt from the standard 100ml liquid restrictions and may be carried through security.

**What if my infant turns 2 during the trip?**
The infant's age on the date of the outbound flight determines the fare for the entire round trip. If your child is 1 year old at the time of the outbound departure but turns 2 before the return flight, the infant fare (EUR 25 per segment) still applies to both directions, provided the booking was made as a round trip. If booked as separate one-way tickets, the appropriate fare for each travel date applies.

### 3.2 Children (2-11 Years)

**Do children aged 2-11 pay full fare?**
Children aged 2-11 years are charged the standard adult fare and receive their own seat and full baggage allowance. SkyItalia does not offer a separate child discount on published fares, though promotional offers occasionally include family pricing. Children aged 2-4 must be accompanied by an adult (18+) at all times during travel.

**Can a child aged 2-4 sit alone in a row?**
No. Children aged 2-4 must be seated directly adjacent to their accompanying adult. During booking and check-in, the system will automatically attempt to seat the child and adult together. If you experience difficulty with seat assignments, contact our customer service team.

### 3.3 Unaccompanied Minors (UMNR)

**Can children fly alone?**
Children aged **5 to 11** can fly unaccompanied with our mandatory Unaccompanied Minor (UMNR) service, which costs **EUR 50 per flight segment**. This includes dedicated staff assistance from check-in to arrival. Children under 5 must travel with an adult (18+).

For children aged **12 to 17**, the UMNR service is optional but recommended. Teens aged 12-17 may travel alone without the service if preferred, but parents or guardians who wish for additional supervision can request it at the same rate of EUR 50 per flight segment.

**What does the Unaccompanied Minor service include?**
The service provides:
- **Dedicated SkyItalia staff** escort from the check-in counter through security, to the departure gate, and onto the aircraft.
- **Cabin crew supervision** throughout the flight, including assistance with meals and comfort.
- **Arrival escort** from the aircraft to the arrivals hall, where the child is handed to the designated pickup person.
- A **fun travel kit** with age-appropriate activities (coloring book, snacks, and a SkyItalia Junior Wings pin).
- **Real-time notifications** to parents/guardians at key milestones: cleared security, boarded, departed, landed, picked up.

**What documents are required for an unaccompanied minor?**
The following documents are required and must be presented at check-in:
1. A valid travel document for the child (passport or national ID card, as applicable).
2. The completed **UMNR Request Form** (available on skyitalia.com/umnr or at the check-in counter), which must include:
   - Full name and contact details of the person dropping off the child at departure.
   - Full name, contact details, and a copy of a photo ID of the person picking up the child at arrival.
   - Emergency contact information.
3. For international flights: any required visa or travel authorization for the child.
4. For children traveling between divorced or separated parents: a notarized parental consent letter may be required, depending on the destination country. Check specific requirements for your route.

**Are there restrictions on connections for unaccompanied minors?**
Yes. Unaccompanied minors aged 5-11 are only accepted on **direct flights** (no connections). SkyItalia does not accept unaccompanied minors on flights that require a change of aircraft or a stopover. For children aged 12-17 using the optional UMNR service, one connection is permitted, provided the layover time is between 1 hour and 3 hours and both flights are operated by SkyItalia (no codeshare or interline connections).

**Can I book the UMNR service online?**
The UMNR service cannot be booked online. You must contact SkyItalia customer service by phone at least **48 hours before departure** to arrange the service and submit the required documentation. Walk-up UMNR requests at the airport are accepted subject to availability but are not guaranteed. We strongly recommend booking in advance.

**What happens if the flight is delayed or cancelled and my child is an unaccompanied minor?**
SkyItalia staff will remain with the child at all times during any disruption. Parents/guardians will be notified immediately by phone. If the flight is cancelled and no same-day alternative is available, SkyItalia will arrange supervised accommodation and meals at airline expense. The child will not be released to anyone other than the designated pickup person listed on the UMNR form.

## 4. Pets and Animals

### 4.1 Pets in the Cabin

**Can I bring my pet on board?**
**Small pets** (dogs, cats, and small domestic animals such as rabbits) in a carrier are allowed in the cabin if the carrier fits under the seat in front of you (max **46 x 28 x 24 cm**) and the total weight (pet + carrier) does not exceed **8 kg**. Fee: **EUR 40** per flight.

**What type of carrier is required?**
The carrier must be:
- Leak-proof and well-ventilated on at least two sides.
- Made of soft or semi-rigid material (hard-shell kennels are not permitted in the cabin).
- Equipped with a secure closure that prevents the pet from escaping.
- Small enough to fit entirely under the seat in front of you (maximum dimensions: **46 x 28 x 24 cm**).

The pet must be able to stand, turn around, and lie down comfortably within the carrier. The carrier counts as your personal item; you may still bring one carry-on bag in addition to the pet carrier.

**How many pets are allowed per passenger?**
One pet per passenger is allowed in the cabin. A maximum of **3 pets** in the cabin are permitted per flight (on a first-come, first-served basis). You must declare your pet at the time of booking or at least 48 hours before departure.

**Are there any breeds not allowed in the cabin?**
Brachycephalic (short-nosed) breeds such as Pugs, French Bulldogs, English Bulldogs, Pekingese, Shih Tzus, Boston Terriers, and similar breeds are permitted in the cabin year-round, provided they meet the weight and carrier size requirements. However, these breeds are subject to restrictions in the cargo hold (see section 4.2).

Aggressive or potentially dangerous breeds as classified by Italian law (e.g., Pit Bull Terrier, Rottweiler, Dogo Argentino, Fila Brasileiro) must be muzzled inside the carrier at all times and require a behavioral assessment certificate from a licensed veterinarian. Check with SkyItalia customer service at the time of booking.

### 4.2 Pets in the Cargo Hold

**What are the rules for pets in the cargo hold?**
**Larger pets** that do not meet cabin requirements must travel in the **cargo hold** in an IATA-approved rigid kennel. Fee: **EUR 80** per flight. Maximum weight (pet + kennel): **75 kg**.

The kennel must be:
- IATA Live Animals Regulations (LAR) compliant.
- Large enough for the pet to stand, turn around, and lie down.
- Equipped with secure metal bolts (not plastic clips) and a water dish attached inside.
- Labeled with "Live Animal" stickers and an arrow indicating the upright position.
- Lined with absorbent material.

**Are brachycephalic breeds allowed in the cargo hold?**
**Not allowed**: brachycephalic breeds (e.g., Bulldogs, Pugs, Boston Terriers, Boxers, Pekingese, Cavalier King Charles Spaniels, and similar flat-nosed breeds) are prohibited from traveling in the cargo hold during **summer months (June through September)** due to elevated health risks from temperature and pressure sensitivity. During the rest of the year (October through May), brachycephalic breeds may travel in the cargo hold provided a veterinary fitness-to-fly certificate is presented, issued within 72 hours of departure.

**Are there temperature restrictions for cargo hold pet travel?**
Yes. SkyItalia will not transport animals in the cargo hold when the ground temperature at departure or arrival airport exceeds **30 degrees Celsius** or falls below **-5 degrees Celsius**. During extreme weather advisories, cargo pet transport may be suspended without notice. The cargo hold used for animal transport is temperature-controlled and pressurized (maintained between 15-25 degrees Celsius during flight).

**Are there seasonal blackout dates for pet cargo transport?**
In addition to summer restrictions for brachycephalic breeds, SkyItalia may impose temporary embargoes on all pet cargo transport during peak holiday periods (typically December 20-January 5 and August 1-20) on selected high-traffic routes. These embargoes are announced at least 30 days in advance on skyitalia.com/pettravel.

### 4.3 Service and Guide Dogs

**Can I bring my guide or service dog on board?**
**Service and guide dogs** (including hearing dogs and psychiatric service dogs) are allowed in the cabin **free of charge** with proper documentation. The dog must be harnessed, well-behaved, and remain at the passenger's feet or under the seat. Service dogs are exempt from the carrier requirement and the 8 kg weight limit.

Required documentation:
- Proof of training from a recognized service dog organization.
- Veterinary health certificate (issued within 30 days of travel).
- EU Pet Passport with up-to-date rabies vaccination (for international travel).
- For flights to/from the United States: a U.S. Department of Transportation form completed by the handler.

**Are emotional support animals accepted?**
SkyItalia recognizes trained psychiatric service dogs but does not accept emotional support animals (ESAs) that do not have formal service dog training credentials. ESAs that do not meet service dog requirements must travel as standard pets (in a carrier, subject to weight limits and applicable fees).

### 4.4 Exotic and Non-Standard Pets

**Can I travel with birds, ferrets, or other unusual pets?**
Small birds (e.g., parakeets, canaries) may travel in the cabin in a covered carrier that meets the standard cabin carrier dimensions. Ferrets are accepted on domestic Italian flights and EU flights where the destination country permits entry. Reptiles, amphibians, rodents (other than hamsters and guinea pigs), arachnids, and insects are not permitted on SkyItalia flights in any cabin. For any unusual pet requests, contact customer service at least 7 days before travel.

## 5. On-Board Services

### 5.1 Cabin Classes and Seat Map

**What cabin classes does SkyItalia offer?**
SkyItalia offers three cabin classes:
- **Business Class** (rows 1-3 on A320neo/A321neo; rows 1-5 on Boeing 787): Seats in a 2-2 configuration on narrow-body aircraft and 1-2-1 on the Boeing 787. Seat pitch: 40 inches (narrow-body) / 60 inches lie-flat (Boeing 787). Seat width: 21 inches (narrow-body) / 22 inches (wide-body).
- **Premium Economy** (rows 6-10 on A320neo/A321neo; rows 10-18 on Boeing 787): Seats in a 3-3 configuration (narrow-body) or 2-3-2 (Boeing 787). Seat pitch: 34 inches. Seat width: 18 inches (narrow-body) / 19 inches (wide-body).
- **Economy** (rows 11-31 on A320neo; rows 11-36 on A321neo; rows 20-56 on Boeing 787): Seats in a 3-3 configuration (narrow-body) or 3-3-3 (Boeing 787). Seat pitch: 30 inches. Seat width: 17.5 inches (narrow-body) / 17.5 inches (wide-body).

**What is the seat configuration on the Boeing 737-800?**
The Boeing 737-800 is operated on select short-haul routes and features two cabin classes: Business (rows 1-3, 2-2 configuration) and Economy (rows 4-33, 3-3 configuration). There is no Premium Economy cabin on the 737-800. Seat pitch in Economy is 30 inches; Business is 38 inches.

### 5.2 Meals and Catering

**Are meals included?**
- **Business Class**: Full meal service included. On flights over 2 hours, a hot meal is served with a choice of two entrees, accompanied by a starter, bread, dessert, and a full bar (including wines selected by our in-house sommelier). On shorter flights, premium snack boxes and complimentary beverages (including alcohol) are provided.
- **Premium Economy**: Light meal or snack box included, plus complimentary beverages (soft drinks, coffee, tea, juice, and one complimentary alcoholic beverage on flights over 2 hours).
- **Economy**: Meals and snacks available for **purchase** on board from the Sky Cafe menu. Water is complimentary. Coffee and tea are available for EUR 2.50.

**What is on the Sky Cafe menu?**
The menu varies by route type:

*Short-haul flights (under 3 hours):*
- Panini and wraps: EUR 6.50 - EUR 8.00
- Snack boxes (cheese and crackers, nuts, fruit): EUR 5.00
- Sweet treats (croissant, brownie, biscotti): EUR 3.00 - EUR 4.50
- Soft drinks, juice, coffee, tea: EUR 2.50
- Beer, wine, spirits: EUR 5.00 - EUR 8.00

*Long-haul flights (3+ hours):*
- Hot meals (pasta, chicken, vegetarian): EUR 12.00 - EUR 15.00
- Fresh salads: EUR 8.00
- Cheese and charcuterie board: EUR 10.00
- Premium snack boxes: EUR 7.00
- Full beverage selection including cocktails: EUR 5.00 - EUR 10.00

**Can I pre-order a special meal?**
Business Class and Premium Economy passengers may pre-order from a curated menu up to 48 hours before departure. Special dietary meals (vegetarian, vegan, gluten-free, kosher, halal, low-sodium, child meal) are available free of charge in Business Class and Premium Economy if requested at least 24 hours before departure. In Economy, special dietary requirements can be accommodated through the pre-purchase menu; allergen information is listed on the Sky Cafe menu cards on board.

**Is there a meal service on very short flights (under 1 hour)?**
On flights under 1 hour (e.g., Milan to Rome), a limited snack and beverage service is offered. Business Class passengers receive a complimentary premium snack box and beverages. Economy passengers may purchase from a streamlined menu (drinks and packaged snacks only).

### 5.3 Wi-Fi and Connectivity

**Is there Wi-Fi on board?**
Yes. Wi-Fi is available on all flights operated by **Airbus A320neo**, **Airbus A321neo**, and **Boeing 787** aircraft. The Boeing 737-800 fleet does not currently have Wi-Fi installed; connectivity upgrades for this fleet are scheduled to be completed by the end of 2027.

**What are the Wi-Fi plans and pricing?**

| Plan | Price | Duration | Features |
|------|-------|----------|----------|
| **Basic** | **EUR 4.99** | 1 hour | Messaging apps (WhatsApp, iMessage, Telegram), email, light web browsing. No streaming. |
| **Standard** | **EUR 9.99** | Full flight | Full internet access, social media, email, web browsing. Video calls supported on long-haul flights. No streaming services. |
| **Premium** | **EUR 14.99** | Full flight | Everything in Standard plus streaming video (Netflix, Disney+, YouTube, etc.) and large file downloads. Best for long-haul entertainment. |

- **Business Class**: Complimentary high-speed Wi-Fi equivalent to the Premium plan is included for all Business Class passengers.
- **SkyMiles Platinum members**: Complimentary Standard Wi-Fi on all flights, regardless of cabin class.
- **SkyMiles Gold members**: 20% discount on all Wi-Fi plans.

**How do I connect to the Wi-Fi?**
Once the aircraft reaches cruising altitude and the captain activates the Wi-Fi signal:
1. Connect to the "SkyItalia_WiFi" network on your device.
2. Open your browser — you will be automatically redirected to the Wi-Fi portal.
3. Select your plan and enter payment details (credit card or PayPal), or enter your SkyMiles number for complimentary/discounted access.
4. Accept the terms and conditions.
5. Browse freely.

**Can I purchase Wi-Fi in advance?**
Yes. You can add a Wi-Fi package to your booking at skyitalia.com/manage or during online check-in. Pre-purchased Wi-Fi packages are 10% cheaper than on-board pricing.

### 5.4 In-Flight Entertainment

**Is there entertainment on board?**
- **Long-haul flights (Boeing 787)**: Every seat is equipped with a seatback personal screen (12-inch in Economy, 15-inch in Premium Economy, 17-inch in Business Class) featuring over 200 movies, 500 TV episodes, music, games, and a live flight map. Noise-canceling headphones are provided in Business Class and Premium Economy; earbuds are provided in Economy.
- **Short-haul flights (A320neo, A321neo, 737-800)**: Streaming entertainment is available via the **SkyItalia app** (download before boarding). Content includes a selection of 50+ movies, 100+ TV episodes, and music channels. Connect your own headphones via Bluetooth or 3.5mm jack.

**Is there a kids' entertainment section?**
Yes. Both the seatback system (long-haul) and the streaming app (short-haul) include a dedicated "SkyKids" section with age-appropriate cartoons, animated movies, educational games, and coloring activities. Parental controls are available.

### 5.5 Power Outlets and USB Ports

**Are there power outlets on board?**
Power outlet availability depends on the aircraft type:

| Aircraft | Business Class | Premium Economy | Economy |
|----------|---------------|-----------------|---------|
| Boeing 787 | AC power outlet (110V) + USB-A + USB-C at every seat | AC power outlet + USB-A at every seat | USB-A + USB-C at every seat |
| Airbus A321neo | AC power outlet + USB-A + USB-C at every seat | USB-A + USB-C at every seat | USB-A at every seat (no AC outlet) |
| Airbus A320neo | AC power outlet + USB-A + USB-C at every seat | USB-A at every seat | USB-A at every seat (no AC outlet) |
| Boeing 737-800 | AC power outlet + USB-A at every seat | N/A (no Premium Economy) | No power outlets or USB ports |

We recommend bringing a portable battery pack for flights on the 737-800 fleet if you need to charge your devices.

### 5.6 Cabin Temperature and Comfort

**Is the cabin temperature adjustable?**
Cabin temperatures are centrally controlled and typically maintained between 22-24 degrees Celsius. On the Boeing 787, individual air nozzles above each seat allow passengers to adjust their personal airflow. Blankets are available upon request in all classes on flights over 2 hours (provided free in Business Class and Premium Economy; available for purchase at EUR 5 in Economy on long-haul flights).

## 6. Seating

**Can I choose my seat?**
- **Business Class**: Free seat selection at booking.
- **Premium Economy**: Free seat selection at booking.
- **Economy Flex**: Free seat selection at booking.
- **Economy Standard**: Seat selection from **EUR 5 to EUR 15** depending on the seat location and flight.
- **Economy Light**: Seat selection from **EUR 8 to EUR 20**. Seats are assigned automatically at check-in if not selected.

**What are extra-legroom seats?**
Exit row and front-row seats with additional legroom (up to 20 cm extra). Available for **EUR 15-35** depending on the flight. Silver+ SkyMiles members can select extra-legroom seats at check-in for free (subject to availability).

**Who cannot sit in exit row seats?**
For safety reasons, passengers seated in exit rows must be at least 18 years old, physically able to assist in an emergency evacuation, and able to understand crew instructions in Italian or English. The following passengers may not occupy exit row seats: unaccompanied minors, passengers traveling with infants or small children, passengers with reduced mobility, passengers who require a seatbelt extension, and passengers who do not meet the language comprehension requirement. If assigned to an exit row and found ineligible, you will be reseated and the exit row fee refunded.

**Can I sit next to my travel companion?**
SkyItalia will make every effort to seat passengers on the same booking together, but this is not guaranteed, especially for Economy Light fares where seats are auto-assigned. To guarantee adjacent seats, we recommend selecting seats at the time of booking. If you are separated from a child under 12 on the same booking, please alert a cabin crew member after boarding and they will attempt to reseat you together.

**Can I request a specific seat type (window, aisle, middle)?**
During booking or check-in, the seat map shows the position of each seat (window, middle, aisle). You can filter or select based on your preference. For fare types that include free seat selection, simply choose your preferred position. For fare types with paid seat selection, the seat map will show the price for each available seat.

## 7. Delays, Cancellations, and Disruptions

### 7.1 Delays

**What happens if my flight is delayed?**
If the delay exceeds **2 hours**, SkyItalia provides refreshments (water and a snack). If it exceeds **5 hours**, you may choose a full refund. If the delay at arrival exceeds **3 hours**, you may be entitled to compensation under EU Regulation 261/2004 (see our Compensation Policy for detailed amounts and eligibility criteria).

**Will I be notified of a delay?**
SkyItalia sends real-time delay notifications via the SkyItalia app (push notification), email, and SMS to the contact information on your booking. Notifications are sent as soon as the delay is confirmed by flight operations. At the airport, updated departure times are displayed on flight information screens and announced over the public address system.

**What refreshments are provided during a delay?**
For delays of 2 hours or more, SkyItalia provides meal vouchers redeemable at airport restaurants and cafes. The voucher value depends on the delay duration and time of day: EUR 8 for snacks (delays of 2-4 hours), EUR 15 for a meal (delays of 4+ hours during meal times). Business Class passengers have access to the SkyItalia lounge during delays, where complimentary food and beverages are available.

**My delay means I will miss my connecting flight. What should I do?**
If both flights are on the same booking (same booking reference), SkyItalia will automatically rebook you on the next available connection at no charge. Your baggage will be re-tagged automatically. If your flights are on separate bookings, SkyItalia is not responsible for the missed connection, and you must contact the second airline directly. We strongly recommend booking through-connections on a single booking to ensure protection.

### 7.2 Cancellations

**What if my flight is cancelled?**
You will be offered rerouting on the next available flight or a full refund. Depending on the notice period and the cause of cancellation, monetary compensation may also apply. See our **Compensation Policy** for full details on compensation amounts, extraordinary circumstances, and the claims process.

**How quickly will I be rebooked after a cancellation?**
SkyItalia aims to rebook affected passengers on the next available SkyItalia flight to the same destination. If no SkyItalia flight is available within **24 hours**, you may be rebooked on a partner airline at no extra cost. Business Class and Platinum SkyMiles members are prioritized for rebooking.

**If my flight is cancelled, can I be rebooked to a different nearby airport?**
Yes. If rerouting to an alternative airport in the same city or region is offered (e.g., from MXP Malpensa to LIN Linate, or from FCO Fiumicino to CIA Ciampino), SkyItalia will cover ground transportation to your original destination airport or city center.

### 7.3 Compensation Overview

**Am I entitled to financial compensation?**
Financial compensation under EU Regulation 261/2004 depends on the circumstances. In general:
- **Delays of 3+ hours at arrival**: Compensation may apply (EUR 250 for flights under 1,500 km; EUR 400 for 1,500-3,500 km; EUR 600 for over 3,500 km).
- **Cancellations with less than 14 days' notice**: Compensation may apply unless the airline offered suitable alternative transport.
- **Denied boarding**: Compensation applies immediately.
- **Extraordinary circumstances**: No compensation owed (severe weather, ATC restrictions, security threats, third-party strikes).

For complete details, including reduced compensation scenarios, claim procedures, and escalation paths, please consult our full **Compensation Policy** document.

### 7.4 During Disruptions

**Who do I contact during a disruption?**
- **At the airport**: SkyItalia service desk in the departure hall. During irregular operations, additional staff are deployed in the gate areas.
- **By phone**: SkyItalia customer service (see your booking confirmation for the number). Wait times may be longer during disruptions; we recommend using the app or chat.
- **Via app**: Real-time updates and self-service rebooking options are available in the SkyItalia app. You can also request refunds and meal vouchers directly through the app.
- **Social media**: @SkyItaliaAir on Twitter/X for quick responses during disruptions.

**Will SkyItalia provide a hotel if I am stranded overnight?**
If a delay or cancellation requires an overnight stay and no suitable rerouting is available the same day, SkyItalia will arrange and pay for hotel accommodation and airport transfers. This applies to delays exceeding 5 hours when no alternative flight is available before the next morning. In extreme cases (full hotels, remote airports), SkyItalia will reimburse reasonable hotel expenses upon presentation of receipts (up to EUR 120 per night per passenger).

## 8. Payments and Invoicing

### 8.1 Accepted Payment Methods

**What payment methods do you accept?**
SkyItalia accepts the following payment methods for bookings on skyitalia.com and the mobile app:
- **Credit and debit cards**: Visa, Mastercard, American Express.
- **Digital wallets**: PayPal, Apple Pay, Google Pay.
- **Installment payments**: Klarna (available for bookings over **EUR 200**; split into 3 interest-free payments).
- **Bank transfer**: Accepted for bookings made more than **14 days** before departure. Transfer instructions are provided at checkout; the booking is held for 48 hours pending payment confirmation.
- **SkyMiles points**: Can be used partially or fully (10,000 points = EUR 100; minimum redemption 5,000 points = EUR 50). Points can be combined with any of the above payment methods.

**Are there additional fees for specific payment methods?**
No surcharges are applied for credit card, debit card, PayPal, Apple Pay, or Google Pay payments within the EU, in compliance with EU Payment Services Directive (PSD2) regulations. Payments in non-EUR currencies may incur currency conversion fees charged by your bank or card issuer — SkyItalia does not control or receive these fees.

### 8.2 Currencies

**In which currencies can I pay?**
The SkyItalia website displays prices in EUR by default. Prices can also be displayed and paid in GBP, USD, CHF, and SEK. The exchange rate applied is the rate at the time of booking and is clearly displayed before payment confirmation. On-board purchases are processed in EUR only; card payments on board are accepted in any currency (conversion at your card issuer's rate).

### 8.3 Invoices and Receipts

**Can I get an invoice for my flight?**
Yes. Request an invoice via your booking management page at skyitalia.com or by contacting customer service. Invoices are sent via email within **5 business days**.

**What information is included on the invoice?**
The invoice includes: passenger name(s), booking reference, flight details (route, date, flight number), fare breakdown (base fare, taxes, surcharges, ancillary services), payment method, total amount paid, and SkyItalia's VAT details (for Italian VAT invoices — fattura elettronica — provide your codice fiscale or partita IVA and SDI/PEC details when requesting).

**Can I get a receipt for on-board purchases?**
Yes. For on-board credit/debit card transactions, a digital receipt is sent to the email address linked to your booking within 24 hours. Cash transactions on board are accompanied by a printed receipt from the cabin crew POS terminal. If you need a formal invoice for on-board purchases, contact customer service with your booking reference and transaction details.

**Can I request an invoice for my company (business travel)?**
Yes. Corporate invoices can be issued with your company's full legal name, VAT number, and registered address. Indicate this during the booking process in the "Invoice" section, or contact customer service after booking. For companies enrolled in the SkyItalia Corporate Program, invoices are generated automatically and sent to the designated finance contact monthly.

### 8.4 Split Payments

**Can I split the payment across multiple methods?**
Partial split payments are supported in the following combinations:
- **SkyMiles points + credit card**: Use points for part of the fare and pay the remainder by card.
- **Klarna + credit card**: Not supported. Klarna must cover the full booking amount.
- **Two different credit cards**: Not supported on the website. However, you may contact our call center, which can process a booking with up to two credit cards.
- **Bank transfer + card**: Not supported.

### 8.5 Refunds

**How long do refunds take?**
Refund processing times depend on the payment method:
- **Credit/debit card**: 7-14 business days (may appear on your next billing cycle).
- **PayPal**: 3-5 business days.
- **Bank transfer**: 14-21 business days.
- **SkyMiles points**: Points restored to your account within 48 hours.
- **Klarna**: Refund is processed through Klarna directly; outstanding installments are cancelled automatically. Allow 5-10 business days for paid installments to be returned.

### 8.6 Price Guarantees and Fare Lock

**Does SkyItalia offer a fare lock option?**
Yes. At the time of booking, you can pay a non-refundable **Fare Lock fee of EUR 15** (EUR 25 for Business Class) to hold your current fare for **48 hours** without committing to full payment. This is useful when you need time to confirm travel plans, gather traveler information, or obtain approval from your employer. The fare and availability are guaranteed during the lock period. If you do not complete the booking within 48 hours, the lock expires and the fee is not refunded.

## 9. Accessibility and Special Assistance

### 9.1 General Information

**Does SkyItalia provide assistance for passengers with disabilities or reduced mobility?**
Yes. SkyItalia is committed to providing equal access to air travel for all passengers. Assistance is available free of charge for passengers with physical, sensory, cognitive, or age-related disabilities. Please notify us of your assistance needs at least **48 hours before departure** (72 hours for certain services such as on-board oxygen or stretcher transport) to ensure everything is arranged. Requests made with less notice will be accommodated on a best-effort basis.

### 9.2 Wheelchair Assistance

**What types of wheelchair assistance are available?**
SkyItalia provides the following categories of wheelchair service (use the appropriate code when booking):
- **WCHR** (Wheelchair - Ramp): Passenger can walk short distances but needs assistance to/from the aircraft (e.g., long airport corridors).
- **WCHS** (Wheelchair - Steps): Passenger cannot climb steps but can move within the cabin to their seat.
- **WCHC** (Wheelchair - Cabin): Passenger is completely immobile and requires assistance to and within the cabin, including transfer to and from their seat.

Personal wheelchairs are transported free of charge in the cargo hold. Electric wheelchairs with lithium-ion batteries are accepted if the battery is under 300 Wh (or two batteries under 160 Wh each). Batteries must be disconnected and terminals protected.

**Can I use my own wheelchair up to the aircraft door?**
Yes. You may remain in your personal wheelchair until the aircraft door. At that point, your wheelchair will be gate-checked and returned to you at the aircraft door upon arrival (or at baggage claim, depending on the airport layout). A narrow aisle wheelchair (aisle chair) will be used to transfer you to your seat.

### 9.3 On-Board Oxygen

**Can I bring supplemental oxygen on board?**
Passengers who require supplemental oxygen during flight must notify SkyItalia at least **72 hours** before departure. You may:
- Use a **SkyItalia-approved portable oxygen concentrator (POC)**: Passengers may bring their own FAA/EASA-approved POC at no charge. Approved brands include Inogen One, Respironics SimplyGo, and Philips EverFlo. The POC must have sufficient battery life for the flight duration plus 50%.
- Request **SkyItalia-supplied on-board oxygen**: Available at a charge of **EUR 150 per flight segment**. Must be requested at least 72 hours before departure. A medical certificate (MEDIF form) signed by your physician is required.

Personal oxygen cylinders (pressurized tanks) are **not permitted** on board in any circumstances.

### 9.4 Visual and Hearing Impairment Assistance

**What assistance is available for visually impaired passengers?**
Visually impaired passengers receive:
- Meet-and-assist from the airport entrance through security, to the gate, and onto the aircraft.
- Priority boarding to familiarize with the seat and surroundings.
- Verbal safety briefing from cabin crew.
- Assistance with meals (description of items and placement).
- Escort from the aircraft to the arrivals hall upon landing.

Guide dogs travel free of charge in the cabin (see Section 4.3 for documentation requirements).

**What assistance is available for hearing-impaired or deaf passengers?**
Hearing-impaired passengers receive:
- Written communication from crew when verbal communication is difficult.
- Visual safety information (safety card review and pointing demonstrations).
- Seat assignments near visual information displays where available.
- Priority notification via visual signals (e.g., illuminated signs for boarding, seatbelt, and landing).
- At the airport, hearing loop systems are available at select SkyItalia counters (Milan MXP, Rome FCO, and London LHR).

### 9.5 Dedicated Seating

**Are there dedicated seats for passengers with disabilities?**
Passengers with reduced mobility are typically assigned seats near the front of the cabin for easier access. Armrests that lift on aisle seats are available on all SkyItalia aircraft to facilitate transfer from a wheelchair. Passengers requiring a stretcher (for medical transport) can be accommodated on select aircraft with advance arrangement (contact our Special Assistance team at least 7 days before travel).

Passengers who are blind or severely visually impaired may not be seated in exit rows, but may be seated in any other position including window seats. Passengers traveling with a service dog should request an aisle seat or bulkhead seat to provide adequate space for the dog.

### 13.5 Pregnancy

**Can I fly while pregnant?**
SkyItalia accepts pregnant passengers up to the end of the **36th week** for single pregnancies and the **32nd week** for multiple pregnancies (twins, triplets, etc.). After the 28th week, a medical certificate (fit-to-fly letter) signed by your physician or midwife is required, dated no more than 7 days before the first flight. The certificate must state your expected due date and confirm there are no complications. For high-risk pregnancies, a MEDIF form may be required — contact our Special Assistance team.

### 13.6 Traveling with Medication

**Can I bring medication on board?**
Yes. Prescription medication may be carried in your hand luggage in reasonable quantities for the duration of your trip. Liquid medication exceeding 100 ml is permitted in the cabin with a supporting doctor's letter or prescription. Medication requiring refrigeration can be carried in a small insulated bag with ice packs (inform cabin crew upon boarding). For controlled substances, check the regulations of your destination country and carry the original prescription with the prescribing physician's name and contact information.

Syringes and EpiPens are permitted in the cabin with a supporting medical letter.
